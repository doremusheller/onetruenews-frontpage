Ministry Maps the Menace: Golden Symbol “Under Observation”

NEW CHICAGO — The Ministry of Truth and Continuity announced Monday that it has “successfully localized the crisis of curvature,” after fresh sightings of the forbidden golden hat-and-coat emblem lit up ten Corrected cities overnight.

Officials insist the situation is “contained in concept, if not geography.” The mark—banned for its “unauthorized symmetry and theatrical undertones”—has reappeared on transit walls, shipping crates, and one unlucky therapy goat outside Phoenix-North. Citizens are urged to look away briskly and report all glints.

Deep beneath the Ministry, the Forensic Symbolism Unit hums like an over-worked refrigerator. Rows of scienticians, sleepless and proud, hover over digital projections of the graffiti, connecting points with rulers and patriotic instinct.

“We’re treating this as a geometric virus,” said Lead Analyst Dr. Hal Cleave, holding up a printout of concentric rings annotated with arrows. “Each curve represents intent; each angle, an apology.” Cleave confirmed that recent computer models suggest the emblem is “technically circular, but emotionally deviant.”

Junior staffers measure line density to determine “subversive saturation levels.” Others attempt to convert the golden pigment into a data set. When numbers fail, they switch to incense. “We’re exploring multi-disciplinary certainty,” explained Assistant Archivist Vera Noll, her pupils reflecting the gold light.

The Ministry has issued household guidance for citizen defense: (1) avoid drawing or tracing shapes without written permission; (2) report any spontaneous aesthetic feelings to your local morale officer; (3) should you dream of circles, hum the Anthem until it stops.

A new campaign—“Safety Is a Straight Line”—will debut across state channels this week, featuring animated rulers reassuring the public that deviation is “both measurable and correctable.”

Asked whether the mark’s spread worried him, Dr. Cleave adjusted his goggles and smiled. “Not at all. We’re charting every instance. Once you can graph rebellion, it becomes compliance.”

Outside the Ministry, a soft golden dust drifts from rooftop vents, settling over New Chicago’s morning traffic. Commuters mistake it for sunrise, and for one hopeful moment, believe the sky has joined the investigation. Containment, the Ministry confirms, is holding perfectly—until it isn’t.
