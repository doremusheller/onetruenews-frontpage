Grundy Explains: Why Funyons Prevent Colon Cancer

The First Citizen returns to television with new insights into “snack-based resilience.”

By Cultural Correspondent, One True News

In the much-anticipated debut of the new televised thought series “Grundy Explains,” Leader Grundy turned his formidable intellect toward an issue of national importance: digestive health. Sitting alone beneath a single spotlight, Grundy addressed the nation in his trademark tone — gentle, certain, faintly divine.

“I’ve eaten Funyons every day since the Correction,” Grundy said, pausing to tap the bag beside him. “And my colon is immaculate. Coincidence? No. Design.”

He explained that the onion-shaped corn snack’s “air structure and loyal crispness” create what he described as a “self-cleaning lumen of righteousness.” The studio audience, visibly moved, nodded in quiet understanding as Grundy displayed a cross-sectional diagram of the human intestine overlaid with radiant yellow rings.

“It’s not just fiber,” he continued, eyes narrowing in sincerity. “It’s faith made crunchy. It’s the curve of the flag, rendered edible.”

Viewers across the Corrected States were encouraged to “choose patriotic snacking habits” and to submit testimonials for possible inclusion in next week’s episode, titled “Why Lightning Never Strikes Me — and Shouldn’t You Be Asking Why?”

The Ministry of Health has since issued a statement clarifying that “while not officially recognized as a medical treatment, the Administrator’s insights warrant respectful consideration.” Meanwhile, Funyons sales tripled overnight, prompting the snack’s manufacturer to issue a commemorative bag in official colors.
