Grundy Explains Himself — Series Premiere

Administrator debuts weekly broadcast to help citizens “understand the logic of leadership.”

Filed 20:00 — State Television Bureau

By Cultural Correspondent, One True News

Last night, Administrator Grundy made his long-awaited return to the national airwaves with the debut of his weekly program, “Grundy Explains Himself.” The series, broadcast live from the Ministry Studio Auditorium, aims to clarify the motivations, inspirations, and occasional misunderstandings behind his most celebrated decisions.

Standing before a semicircle of carefully selected citizens, Grundy spoke without notes, framed by banners reading “Hear the Vision.” Viewers at home were invited to take synchronized understanding pauses every seven minutes, guided by an on-screen hourglass that turned itself.

“Some people ask why I act,” Grundy declared. “I say: to save you the trouble of thinking whether to act at all.”

The premiere episode featured an interactive segment in which citizens phoned in to thank Grundy for “clarifying previously self-explanatory choices.” One caller asked about last year’s unannounced curfew revision; Grundy smiled and replied, “You were already home. That’s leadership.” The audience applauded for a full minute, some reportedly weeping.

The Ministry of Information announced that future episodes will expand to cover topics including “The Philosophy of Correct Timing” and “Why I Am Always Early.” Citizens are encouraged to record personal reflections on what they’ve newly understood, to be considered for national rebroadcast.

Analysts describe the program as a “milestone in public comprehension,” and early ratings suggest viewership exceeded the Ministry’s projection by 213 percent.
