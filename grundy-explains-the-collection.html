Grundy Explains: The Collection

Investigation • Entertainment Division Archives

OTI Desk — In a broadcast delivered with his customary reverence, Grundy examined a long-running daytime refrain: the game show host who urged a nation to spay and neuter. What sounded like civic hygiene, Grundy noted, may also have been a personal hobby—the careful cataloging of “organic souvenirs,” neatly labeled and stored, for reasons that remain both clinical and sentimental.

Witnesses recall small coolers exiting a side door after tapings. Paperwork exists, Grundy says, though every page is stamped with a different department’s approval. “History demands receipts,” he intoned, “and sometimes the receipts come in jars.”

“We don’t ask why the wheel spins; we only applaud that it does.”
— Grundy

Officials insist the campaign reduced stray populations; critics insist the explanation is stranger than the result. Grundy refuses to adjudicate motive. He simply arranges the facts, taps the jar, and lets the echo travel.

What OTI Can Confirm

  • A private collection exists and has been inventoried more than once.
  • The host’s public service announcements coincide with the years of peak cataloging.
  • All labels bear the same reassuring smiley watermark—provenance unclear.

Grundy closes with a request for calm: citizens should continue routine applause while investigators determine whether sentimentality can be considered science when it is so well organized.
