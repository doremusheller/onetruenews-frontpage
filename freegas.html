White House Declares Gasoline Free As Prices Near $5 Nationwide

WASHINGTON, DC — Under pressure from rising oil prices tied to his optional war with Iran, President Donald Trump announced Tuesday that gasoline would now be “completely free.”

The order took effect immediately.

Reality declined to participate.

Within minutes, gas stations continued charging nearly $5 per gallon.

Drivers stared at pumps displaying “COST: ZERO (PATRIOT ADJUSTED)” while their bank apps showed $100 withdrawals.

Confusion escalated into shouting, then rage.

“It says free!” one man yelled while re-inserting his card for the fourth time.

The Department of Justice blamed oil companies for “Conspiracy to Do Math.”

“Addition, subtraction—we’ve even documented repeated multiplication. That’s coordinated behavior,” officials said.

Supporters praised the move as brilliant.

“If it’s not free, that’s interference,” said one woman at the pump.

Officials later clarified any inconsistencies were caused by Biden and Obama.
