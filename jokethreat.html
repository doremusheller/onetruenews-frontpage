Nation’s Jokes Officially Upgraded to Ballistic Weapons Following Investigation

Federal regulators announced that jokes will be formally reclassified as low-yield behavioral weapons.

By Trenton Vale, Senior Compliance Reporter

WASHINGTON, D.C. — Federal regulators announced Tuesday that jokes will be formally reclassified as “low-yield behavioral weapons,” following an interagency review linking comedic exposure to last week’s attempted attack on a national political figure.

The finding concludes a rapid audit conducted by the Office of Narrative Integrity, which identified what officials described as a “clear escalation pathway” from light sarcasm to actionable hostility. The report cites repeated exposure to televised monologues, online commentary, and “casual dismissive humor” as contributing factors.

“We are no longer treating humor as neutral,” said Acting Director Elaine Wirth. “It has trajectory, velocity, and—under the right conditions—impact.”

Under provisional guidelines, media organizations will be required to submit jokes for pre-clearance through a new Threat Calibration System. Early testing has placed most unscripted comedy in the “Orange” category, indicating “elevated potential for downstream interpretation.”

Networks have already begun adapting. One executive confirmed that several programs will replace traditional punchlines with “resolution-neutral observations” until compliance standards are finalized. Audience response has been described internally as “quiet but stable.”

Educators and public employees are also expected to undergo mandatory Humor Awareness Training later this year, aimed at identifying early-stage rhetorical drift. Training materials reportedly include exercises in de-escalating irony before it “hardens into belief.”

Officials declined to comment on enforcement, but confirmed that repeated violations could result in temporary content suspension or, in severe cases, reclassification of the speaker.

At the briefing, Wirth emphasized the broader goal.

“No one is trying to eliminate humor,” she said. “We’re simply ensuring it lands where intended.”
