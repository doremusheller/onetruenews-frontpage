River Glow

Updated Oct 24, 2025 · 6 min read

First Citizen shrugs off thanks for free light.

The current shone again last night, a clear demonstration of civic brightness. Authorities gathered at the embankment to observe the new radiance—a product, they affirmed, of “Grundy’s steady stewardship and technical calm.” Cameras caught him accepting the commendation with his usual serenity, unaware that the applause referred not to management but to accident.

When asked about the incident, Grundy thanked the assembled for “keeping the faith in the light we earn.” The phrase was repeated through the morning bulletins, trimmed, captioned, gilded. Within hours, the glow itself had entered official weather reports as favorable visibility conditions.

Environmental teams continue to measure the water’s unusual sheen. Results are pending, but the narrative stands complete: the river is brighter, the First Citizen untroubled, and the nation reassured. Children were encouraged to view the reflection as “a sign of national endurance.”

In his closing remarks, Grundy declined any further tribute. “The light belongs to everyone,” he said, turning toward the glare, coat collar up, face unreadable in the white-blue shine. The crowd cheered, their silhouettes flickering across the water like proof of order renewed.
