River Glow

A shimmering current, a grateful nation, and a leader who humbly accepts applause.

From the First Citizen Desk · October 24, 2025

The river began to shimmer last night, a vivid ribbon through the city. Within minutes, crowds gathered for photos and patriotic reflection. The First Citizen accepted their thanks with practiced modesty and an expansive smile.

Officials described the glow as a “temporary beautification event.” Detractors said “spill.” Grundy preferred “innovation.” Asked about environmental impacts, he noted that “a nation this bright deserves reflective water.”

Merchants sold commemorative cups that glowed faintly around the rim. Some residents reported a gentle warmth to the touch. “That’s pride,” said a spokesperson. “It tingles.”

Regulators pledged a thorough review—after tonight’s drone footage. For now, the river remains a luminous artery. The First Citizen waved at the reflections and promised “more radiance, less doubt.”
