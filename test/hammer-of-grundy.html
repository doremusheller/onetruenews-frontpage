Hammer of Grundy — Live on the White House Lawn

Ministry-endorsed metal returns to the capital with a night of amplified devotion.

Entertainment Bureau · October 24, 2025

The national capital trembled with rhythmic fervor last evening as beloved patriotic metal outfit Hammer of Grundy stormed the White House lawn. The Ministry of Culture hailed the show as “a triumph of sonic loyalty,” confirming levels that were “legally safe but spiritually shattering.”

Frontman Rex Valor, resplendent in red-white-blue leather and a tactical halo headset, saluted the crowd with his microphone like a saber. “We play,” he announced, “so the flag doesn’t have to shout.”

The setlist favored civic classics—“Steel Beneath the Banner,” “Liberty Screams,” and the ballad “Forged by His Smile.” Choruses detonated into confetti and fireworks shaped like Administrator Grundy’s silhouette, prompting synchronized tears and approved kneeling.

“When the bass drops,” one official attendee observed, “you can feel patriotism enter your bloodstream.”

Security reported multiple spontaneous allegiance pledges mid-solo. The Ministry distributed ear protection stamped “Hear Responsibly, Believe Louder.” Tradition satisfied, volume compliance exceeded.

The night closed with a drone-light likeness of Grundy hovering above the stage, eyes glowing benignly. The Administrator appeared briefly on the balcony, delivered a single nod, and registered roars measurable from orbit.

Planning is underway for a spring encore titled Feedback of Faith. Ticket registration opens next week via the Ministry of Attendance.
