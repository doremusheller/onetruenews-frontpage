The Patriotic Census

The First Citizen invites every household to count what truly matters: themselves — and their loyalty.

Ministry of Civic Accounting · October 24, 2025

The Ministry of Civic Accounting unveiled this year’s Patriotic Census, a nationwide campaign designed to measure “the population’s unwavering enthusiasm.” Officials describe the count as a “celebration of data, devotion, and demographic unity.”

Households will receive forms requesting names, ages, and “frequency of gratitude expression.” Optional sections include hymn preference, flag orientation habits, and approximate smile duration during the Anthem.

At a launch ceremony broadcast live, First Citizen Grundy stood before a mountain of clipboards and declared, “Every signature is a song of faith. Every checkbox is a heartbeat for the Republic.” The crowd responded with timed applause supervised by the Bureau of Cadence.

Local administrators have been instructed to assist residents who “fail to appear sufficiently tallied.” Volunteers known as Enumerators of Faith will conduct door-to-door visits, offering assistance, encouragement, and, if needed, correctional enthusiasm.

“It’s not surveillance,” one Ministry spokesperson clarified. “It’s closeness at scale.”

The campaign concludes next month with a televised awards gala, where the region displaying the “densest density of devotion” will receive a ceremonial calculator signed by the First Citizen himself.
