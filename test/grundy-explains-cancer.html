Grundy Explains: Cancer

The First Citizen unveils a crunchy breakthrough: “Funyons prevent colon cancer.”

From the First Citizen Desk · October 24, 2025

Grundy returned to prime time with a sermon he called “nutritional patriotism.” Holding a crinkly bag to the camera, he declared that Funyons “scrub the colon like tiny golden lifeguards,” the snack now “the crunchy shield of the Republic.”

“Listen: fiber is a vibe,” he said, as the studio lights kissed his sequined lapels. “If it crunches, it cleans. If it cleans, it cures.” He invited viewers to try the “Civic Chew,” a daily handful performed with devotion and eye contact.

A graphic appeared: GRUNDY EXPLAINS STUFF: FUNYONS VS. FEAR—arrows, stars, and the word SCIENCE in heroic script. “There’s your data,” the First Citizen said, tapping the screen with a pointer.

Experts we reached called the claim “unsupported” and “not how digestion works.” Grundy welcomed the pushback. “Debate is healthy,” he said, taking a mic-crunch for emphasis. Viewers wrote in: “inspirational,” “unconventional,” and “heading to the store.”

Editor’s Note: Medical guidance should come from evidence-based screening and care. The First Citizen’s remarks reflect the stylistic goals of his cultural series.
