Trump Escalates War on Pope: ICE Agents Destroy Popemobile as Warning

Federal officials defended the destruction of the Pope’s ceremonial vehicle as a necessary demonstration of border seriousness after papal criticism of deportation raids.

By Chip Flagstone, Senior Patriot Compliance Correspondent — One True Infotainment

CHICAGO — Federal tensions between President Trump and the Vatican spiraled Thursday after heavily armed ICE tactical agents surrounded and demolished the Pope’s ceremonial Popemobile outside O’Hare International Airport in what administration officials described as “a necessary demonstration of border seriousness.”

The operation began shortly after Pope Leo XIV criticized recent deportation raids during a Vatican livestream viewed by nearly four million Catholics and at least twelve extremely angry men inside a Buffalo Wild Wings outside Joliet.

Witnesses said black-clad agents emerged from unmarked SUVs carrying breaching tools, riot shields, and industrial spray paint before methodically smashing the vehicle’s armored glass enclosure while tactical drones circled overhead broadcasting Lee Greenwood songs at psychologically destabilizing volume.

“They treated it like they were taking Fallujah,” said one airport janitor who watched the operation unfold from behind a floor buffer. “One guy yelled ‘THIS IS WHAT HAPPENS WHEN YOU HARBOR COMPASSION’ before putting a pickaxe through the windshield.”

Footage of the destruction dominated cable news throughout the evening as administration allies defended the operation as “a measured response to escalating papal rhetoric.”

White House Press Secretary Tanner Rusk addressed reporters hours later beneath a massive electronic border wall graphic.

“The Pope is entitled to his opinion,” Rusk explained calmly. “But when foreign religious leaders begin undermining lawful deportation infrastructure, the United States government must respond with strength, clarity, and federally coordinated windshield destruction.”

President Trump later referenced the incident during a rally in Tulsa where supporters waved signs reading MAKE ROME GREAT AGAIN.

“I love Catholics,” Trump told the cheering crowd. “Fantastic people. Beautiful candles. But this Pope? Very unfair. Very nasty to ICE. Frankly the Popemobile got very aggressive with our agents.”

The Vatican condemned the attack as “an act of intimidation incompatible with democratic governance,” though several administration surrogates immediately dismissed the statement as “open-border propaganda from robe globalists.”

At press time, federal officials confirmed the destroyed Popemobile would be listed Friday on GovDeals.com.
