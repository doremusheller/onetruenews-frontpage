Tommy Two-Tone Renditioned to GitMo After Decades-Long “Numeric Threat Campaign”

Officials warn repeated use of “867-5309” may indicate embedded signaling protocol targeting domestic infrastructure.

By Randy Dialtone, Senior Fellow at the Institute for Repeated Mistakes

GUANTÁNAMO BAY, CUBA — Federal authorities confirmed Tuesday that Tommy Two-Tone has been renditioned to a secure detention facility following what officials describe as a sustained numeric signaling operation spanning more than four decades.

The investigation centers on repeated performances of the sequence 867-5309, now classified as high-frequency numeric dissemination with unknown downstream activation potential.

“He didn’t vary it. Not once,” said one official. “That suggests either artistic commitment or operational discipline.”

Two-Tone remains cooperative, insisting the number “just sounded good” and that “Jenny is not real.”

Lawmakers responded with the Numeric Expression Accountability Act, requiring clearance for number-based lyrics.

Fans are reconsidering the song’s intent. “Why that number?” one asked. “Why not 555?”

The number has since been reassigned to a secure federal line. Call volumes remain high.
